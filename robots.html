Me and robotics:

For the past two years, I have participated as a software developer on my high school's robotics team, Liquid Oxygen. This past year, our robot was good enough that we won our regional qualifier.

This year's competition:

This year's competition, known as PowerPlay, had us building robots that could place small cones on top of poles of varying heights. Bonus points were also given if the robot was able to operate without human control, if it could identify multicolored cones, and if it could park at the end of the game. To learn more, read the rulebook here. You can find our code from that season here.
